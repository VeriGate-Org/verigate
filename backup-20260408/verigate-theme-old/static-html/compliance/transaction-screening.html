Overview

VeriGate's Transaction Screening service monitors financial transactions in real time, screening counterparties against sanctions lists and detecting suspicious patterns that may indicate money laundering, terrorist financing, or sanctions evasion. Our rules engine is configurable to your institution's risk appetite and regulatory requirements.

The FIC Act requires accountable institutions to report suspicious transactions to the Financial Intelligence Centre. Our transaction screening solution automates the detection of unusual patterns, enabling your compliance team to investigate and file Suspicious Transaction Reports (STRs) promptly.

How It Works

1

Integration

Connect VeriGate to your transaction processing system via API. We support real-time streaming and batch processing for all major payment platforms.

2

Rule Configuration

Configure screening rules based on your risk appetite — transaction thresholds, geographic risk, customer risk rating, velocity checks, and pattern detection.

3

Real-Time Screening

Every transaction is screened against sanctions lists and evaluated against your rule set. Suspicious transactions are flagged instantly.

4

Alert Management

Compliance analysts review flagged transactions in our case management portal, with AI-assisted investigation tools and one-click STR filing.

Key Features

Real-Time Processing

Screen transactions in milliseconds, enabling straight-through processing for clean transactions while instantly flagging suspicious activity.

Configurable Rules Engine

Build custom screening rules combining transaction amount, frequency, geography, customer risk rating, and behavioral patterns.

STR Auto-Generation

Automated Suspicious Transaction Report drafting with pre-populated fields, reducing reporting time from hours to minutes.

Turnaround Time

Real-time

Transaction screening runs in real time with sub-second response times. Alert investigation and STR filing are manual processes supported by our tools.
