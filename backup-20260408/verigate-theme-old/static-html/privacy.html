Privacy Policy

Last updated: 1 March 2026

1. Introduction

VeriGate (Pty) Ltd ("VeriGate", "we", "us", or "our") is committed to protecting the personal information of all individuals whose data we process. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, and share personal information in accordance with the Protection of Personal Information Act, 2013 (POPIA) and other applicable South African legislation.

By using our platform and services, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this Privacy Policy and agree to the collection and use of your information as described herein.

2. Information We Collect

In the course of providing our verification and background screening services, we may collect the following categories of personal information:

  • Personal Identification Information: Full name, identity number, date of birth, gender, contact details (email address, phone number, physical address), and passport or permit details where applicable.
  • Employment and Educational History: Previous employer details, job titles, dates of employment, qualifications obtained, institutions attended, and professional certifications.
  • Financial Information: Credit history and financial records where credit checks are requested and appropriate consent has been obtained.
  • Website Usage Data: IP address, browser type, device information, pages visited, referring URLs, and session duration collected through cookies and analytics tools.

3. How We Use Information

We use the personal information we collect for the following purposes:

  • Processing Verification Requests: To conduct background screening checks as requested by our clients, including identity verification, qualification verification, employment history checks, criminal record checks, and credit checks.
  • Communicating with Clients: To respond to enquiries, provide verification results, send service updates, and deliver customer support.
  • Improving Our Services: To analyse usage patterns, enhance platform functionality, develop new features, and improve the overall quality and efficiency of our verification services.
  • Compliance with Legal Obligations: To comply with applicable laws, regulations, and lawful requests from government authorities, including POPIA, the National Credit Act, and the Criminal Procedure Act.

4. Data Sharing

We do not sell or rent personal information to third parties. We share personal data only in the following circumstances:

  • Verified Institutions: We share relevant personal information with educational institutions, previous employers, and professional bodies solely for the purpose of completing verification requests.
  • Credit Bureaus: Where credit checks are requested and consent has been obtained, we share information with registered credit bureaus in accordance with the National Credit Act.
  • Department of Home Affairs: For identity verification and citizenship confirmation purposes.
  • Legal Requirements: Where required by law, court order, or lawful request from a government authority, we may disclose personal information to the relevant body.

5. Data Security

We take the security of personal information seriously and employ industry-standard measures to protect data against unauthorised access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. These measures include:

  • Encryption: All data transmitted between users and our platform is encrypted using TLS 1.2 or higher. Data at rest is encrypted using AES-256 encryption.
  • Access Controls: Role-based access controls ensure that only authorised personnel can access personal information, with access granted on a need-to-know basis.
  • Regular Security Audits: We conduct regular security assessments, vulnerability scans, and penetration testing to identify and address potential security risks.
  • Infrastructure: Our platform is hosted on secure, SOC 2-compliant infrastructure with redundancy and disaster recovery capabilities.

6. Data Retention

Verification records and associated personal information are retained for a period of five (5) years from the date of the verification, in accordance with regulatory requirements and industry best practices. After this retention period, records are securely destroyed or anonymised.

Website usage data and analytics information is retained for a period of twenty-four (24) months, after which it is automatically deleted.

7. Your Rights

Under POPIA, you have the following rights regarding your personal information:

  • Right of Access: You have the right to request confirmation of whether we hold personal information about you and to request access to that information.
  • Right to Correction: You have the right to request the correction or updating of your personal information if it is inaccurate, incomplete, or misleading.
  • Right to Deletion: You have the right to request the deletion of your personal information where it is no longer necessary for the purpose for which it was collected, subject to any legal retention obligations.
  • Right to Object: You have the right to object to the processing of your personal information on reasonable grounds.
  • Right to Lodge a Complaint: You have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Regulator if you believe your personal information has been mishandled.

8. Contact Us

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy, wish to exercise your rights, or need to report a data protection concern, please contact our Data Protection Officer:

  • Email: privacy@verigate.co.za
  • Address: VeriGate (Pty) Ltd, Cape Town, South Africa
