## 2. AWS Activate Founders Tier

**Status:** ALWAYS OPEN -- Rolling applications

### Application URL
**Apply here:** https://aws.amazon.com/activate/activate-landing/
**Step-by-step guide:** https://aws.amazon.com/startups/learn/applying-for-aws-activate-credits-a-step-by-step-guide

### What You Get
- **Founders Package:** $1,000 in AWS credits (no VC/accelerator affiliation needed)
- **Portfolio Package:** Up to $100,000 in AWS credits (requires affiliation with an AWS Activate Provider -- accelerator/VC)
- Credits for compute, storage, ML, databases, etc.
- AWS technical support credits
- Training resources

### Eligibility
- Self-funded or pre-Series B startup
- Founded within the past 10 years
- Fully functioning company website (NOT a "coming soon" page)
- AWS account on a Paid Tier Plan
- Must not have previously received equal or greater AWS Activate credits

### Required Documents / Information
- Company name and details
- Business email address (MUST match your company domain -- personal emails get rejected)
- Company website URL
- Product description and target market
- Current funding stage and date of most recent round
- AWS Account ID

### Step-by-Step Process
1. **Create an AWS Builder ID** using a personal email -- verify via confirmation email
2. **Complete AWS Activate Profile** -- use your BUSINESS email (e.g., info@shumelahire.com)
3. **Select your package** -- Founders ($1,000) or Portfolio (up to $100K, needs Org ID from accelerator)
4. **Provide startup details** -- business info, product description, target market, funding stage
5. **Link your AWS Account** -- needs admin permissions, enable pop-ups
6. **Verify AWS Account** in AWS Management Console -- look for "Accounts linked successfully"
7. **Review and submit** -- double-check everything
8. Wait 7-10 business days for decision
9. Credits auto-deposit within 3-4 hours of approval

### Common Rejection Reasons
1. Email domain mismatch (personal email vs. business email)
2. Suspended or inactive AWS account
3. Previously received equal/greater credits
4. Unverifiable startup details or company name inconsistencies
5. Website not live, "coming soon" pages, or no clear software product description
6. Failed internal business review

### Tips
- Use a corporate email (tech@yourstartup.com), NOT gmail/outlook
- Your website must be live, load quickly, and clearly describe a software product
- Track credit expiration dates (typically 1-2 years) -- they do NOT roll over
- Credits do not retroactively cover past bills
- Apply for Founders first, then upgrade to Portfolio when you join an accelerator

### Contact
- AWS Activate Support via AWS Console
- https://aws.amazon.com/startups/credits

### Sources
- [AWS Activate Application Guide](https://aws.amazon.com/startups/learn/applying-for-aws-activate-credits-a-step-by-step-guide)

## 3. TIA Seed Fund (via TuksNovation and other implementing partners)

**Status:** OPEN via implementing partners -- check individual partner deadlines

### Application URL
- **TuksNovation (UP-based):** https://tuksnovation.co.za/innovation-challenge-2025-2026/
- **TIA main site:** https://www.tia.org.za/seed-fund/
- **SAMRC partnership:** https://www.samrc.ac.za/funding/tia-seed-fund-programme
- **TIA application checklist:** https://www.tia.org.za/application-checklist/

### What You Get
- **Pre-Seed Fund:** For proof of concept, prototype development, market research (TRL3-4)
- **Seed Fund:** For advanced-stage innovations -- optimize tech, achieve MVP, meet regulatory requirements, prepare for market entry
- Non-dilutive grant funding (not equity)
- Mentorship through implementing partner (e.g., TuksNovation incubation)

### Eligibility
- South African registered SMME
- Priority given to SMMEs incubated through TIA implementing partners (TuksNovation, SAMRC, Savant, etc.)
- Not insolvent or under debt review
- Valid tax clearance status
- Registered with CIPC
- No shareholding by public servants
- Registered on Central Supplier Database (CSD)
- All primary technology activities must occur within South Africa
- Priority given to previously disadvantaged youth, women, and persons with disabilities

### Required Documents
- CIPC registration documents
- Tax clearance certificate
- CSD registration
- Business plan
- Technology development plan
- B-BBEE certificate/affidavit
- Valid ID documents (SA citizen or permanent resident)
- Application form (available from implementing partner)

### Step-by-Step Process
1. **Identify your implementing partner** -- TuksNovation (Pretoria), SAMRC (health tech), Savant, etc.
2. **Contact the implementing partner** to confirm current call for applications
3. TIA uses a three-stage process:
   a. **Statement of Interest** and basic assessment
   b. **Full assessment** (detailed application)
   c. **Approval** by TIA Investment Committee
4. Applications adjudicated on merit using TIA Seed Fund Pre-Seed/Seed framework

### Tips
- You CANNOT apply directly to TIA for the Seed Fund -- you MUST go through an implementing partner
- Being incubated at TuksNovation gives you priority access
- Focus on demonstrating technology readiness level (TRL) progression
- GovTech/RegTech fits well under "innovations with enhanced market potential"
- Check multiple implementing partners -- some may have open calls when others don't

### Contact

## 4. DTIC SPII (Support Programme for Industrial Innovation)

**Status:** ALWAYS OPEN -- Rolling applications

### Application URL
- **Main page:** https://www.thedtic.gov.za/financial-and-non-financial-support/incentives/support-programme-for-industrial-innovation-spii/
- **Application forms:** Download from DTIC website (link on the page above)
- **Email submission:** spiiapplications@thedtic.gov.za

### What You Get
- **Matching Scheme:** Up to 50% of qualifying development costs, maximum R5 million grant
- **Partnership Scheme:** Levy-based grant -- percentage of sales over a fixed number of years
- Covers development phase only (from end of basic research to pre-production prototype)

### Eligibility
- South African registered company
- Must be developing innovative products and/or processes
- Project must be in the "development phase" (not basic research, not production)
- Must demonstrate commercial viability

### Required Documents
- SPII application form (download from DTIC website)
- B-BBEE Declaration/Affidavit
- Declaration by consultant and applicant
- Full project description and technical details
- Financial projections and cost breakdowns
- Company registration documents

### Step-by-Step Process
1. Download the relevant application form and guidelines from the DTIC website
2. Complete the application form with full project and financial details
3. Gather supporting documents (B-BBEE, declarations, financials)
4. Submit completed application to: spiiapplications@thedtic.gov.za
5. DTIC reviews and may request additional information
6. Committee assessment and decision

### Tips
- This is specifically for the DEVELOPMENT phase -- not research, not production
- "Development" = conclusion of basic research through to pre-production prototype
- GovTech/RegTech software development could qualify if framed as innovative product development
- The Matching Scheme (up to R5M) is more relevant than the Partnership Scheme for tech startups
- Get a consultant familiar with DTIC applications -- the forms are complex

### Contact
- **SPII Enquiries:** spiienquiries@thedti.gov.za
- **Phone:** +27 (12) 394 5953 / 1811 (Sathiya Arumugasamy / Moshiane Maseko)
