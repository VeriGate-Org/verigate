01 — The Problem

Fragmented, Manual Verification

Verifying a person or company's identity, tax compliance, property ownership, qualifications, and creditworthiness in South Africa requires querying multiple disconnected government and private systems — DHA, CIPC, SARS, the Deeds Office, SAQA, credit bureaus, and more. The process is manual, slow, error-prone, and riddled with fraud vulnerabilities.

✓
02 — The Solution

Orchestrated Risk Intelligence

VeriGate routes verification requests to 14+ authoritative sources, returning standardised results with built-in AI-powered risk scoring, fraud detection, and full audit trails. Each agency retains complete control of its own data — VeriGate orchestrates, never centralises. Data sovereignty is guaranteed by architecture, not by policy. By cross-referencing CIPC directorship records, shareholding data, sanctions watchlists, property ownership, and PEP databases in real time, VeriGate automatically surfaces hidden relationships — such as a procurement officer who is also a director of the bidding supplier, or an undisclosed beneficial ownership link between contracting parties.
