## 1. What Is SITA

### Mandate and Legal Basis

The **State Information Technology Agency (SITA)** is a South African state-owned enterprise established in 1999 under the **SITA Act (No. 88 of 1998)**. It falls under the **Department of Communications and Digital Technologies (DCDT)**.

SITA's mandate is to:

- **Consolidate and coordinate** the State's information technology resources to achieve cost savings through economies of scale
- **Increase delivery capabilities** of IT services to government
- **Enhance interoperability** across government systems
- **Manage the IT procurement and delivery process** to ensure government gets value for money
- **Leverage ICT as a strategic resource** for government, improving public service delivery

### Role in Government IT Procurement

SITA is the **mandatory procurement agency** for all national and provincial government IT requirements. Under the SITA Act and its regulations:

- All government departments must approach SITA first for ICT goods and services procurement
- The Auditor-General treats procurement outside SITA as **irregular expenditure** (though recent amendments have introduced exceptions — see Section 9)
- SITA negotiates **transversal contracts** (bulk agreements) on behalf of all government departments
- SITA manages the entire supply chain: from needs assessment, solicitation, supplier selection, bid adjudication, through to contract administration

### Why Registration Matters

If Arthmatic DevWorks wants to sell VeriGate (its real-time risk intelligence platform) or any IT services to national or provincial government departments, **SITA is the gatekeeper**. Government departments are legally obligated to procure IT through SITA (with limited recent exceptions). Being registered and visible in the correct procurement channels is essential for:

- Being discoverable when departments raise IT requirements through SITA
- Qualifying to respond to SITA RFQs (Requests for Quotation) and RFBs (Requests for Bid)
- Getting listed on transversal contracts or panels, which guarantee ongoing procurement flow
- Meeting compliance requirements that are verified before any contract is awarded

## 2. How to Register as a SITA Supplier

### Key Principle: SITA Does Not Maintain Its Own Supplier Register

A critical finding from this research: **SITA does not certify or register service providers directly**. There is no "SITA supplier registration form" to fill in. Instead, SITA uses the **Central Supplier Database (CSD)** managed by National Treasury as its supplier information source.

### Step-by-Step Process

#### Step 1: Register on the Central Supplier Database (CSD)

This is the foundational requirement. All potential SITA suppliers must self-register at **[secure.csd.gov.za](https://secure.csd.gov.za/)**.

1. **Create a user account** — Go to secure.csd.gov.za and register as a new user with your email address and cell phone number
2. **Create your organisation profile** — Enter your company/business information including:
   - Company registration number (from CIPC)
   - Tax reference number (from SARS)
   - VAT number (if registered)
   - Company directors and shareholders
   - B-BBEE status
   - Banking details
   - Contact information (preferred contact name, email, telephone)
   - Business services/commodities you offer
3. **Submit for verification** — The CSD system automatically verifies your information against:
   - SARS (tax compliance)
   - CIPC (company registration validity)
   - Department of Home Affairs (director ID verification)
   - Your bank (banking details verification)
4. **Receive your CSD number and unique reference code** — Upon successful verification, you receive a **CSD Registration Number** and a **Unique Reference Code**. These are required when responding to any SITA tender.

#### Step 2: Register on SITA's e-Procurement Platform (g-Commerce)

SITA operates the **g-Commerce** platform ([gcommerce.gov.za](https://www.gcommerce.gov.za/)) — a fully integrated source-to-pay solution for government procurement. Additionally, there is the **ERP iSupplier & eCatalogue** platform.

1. Go to the g-Commerce portal
2. Register with your username and password
3. Complete the supplier profile, linking to your CSD credentials

#### Step 3: Monitor and Respond to Tenders

SITA does not have a pre-qualification or pre-approval step. Instead:

1. **Continuously monitor** tender opportunities at:
   - [www.sita.co.za/tenders](https://www.sita.co.za/content/invitations) (SITA's own tender page)
   - [www.etenders.gov.za](https://www.etenders.gov.za/) (National Treasury's eTender portal)
   - Government Gazette tender bulletins
2. **Respond to relevant RFQs, RFBs, or RFPs** when published
3. Include your **CSD number and unique reference code** in every bid submission

#### Step 4 (Optional): Technology Certification Process (TCP)

If VeriGate includes hardware components or you are an OEM, SITA has a **Technology Certification Process (TCP)**. However, this is primarily for hardware OEMs (PCs, servers, networking equipment, etc.), not for software/SaaS products. The TCP currently covers:

- Personal Computing Devices
- Peripherals
- Servers & Storage
- Networking equipment
- Infrastructure (UPS, racks)
- Audiovisual Communications
- Surveillance & Access Control
- Assistive Technologies
- Education Solutions

**For VeriGate (a software/SaaS verification platform), the TCP is likely not applicable.** TCP contact: tas@sita.co.za

## 3. Registration Requirements

### Documents and Prerequisites for CSD Registration

| Requirement | Detail |
|---|---|
| **CIPC Registration Certificate** | Valid company registration from the Companies and Intellectual Property Commission |
| **Tax Clearance / TCS Pin** | Valid Tax Compliance Status (TCS) pin from SARS — verified in real-time |
| **Banking Details** | Bank-verified letter or stamped bank confirmation |
| **Director ID Documents** | Certified copies of all directors' South African IDs |
| **B-BBEE Certificate or Affidavit** | B-BBEE verification certificate from an accredited verification agency, OR a sworn affidavit (for EMEs/QSEs with turnover below R50 million) |
| **VAT Registration** | If VAT-registered, your VAT number |
| **Company Contact Details** | Physical address, postal address, email, phone numbers |
| **Service/Commodity Classification** | Select the UNSPSC (United Nations Standard Products and Services Code) categories your company operates in |

### Additional Requirements for Bidding on SITA Tenders

When actually responding to SITA tenders (beyond CSD registration), you will typically need:

- **CSD number and unique reference code** (mandatory on every bid)
- **Completed SBD (Standard Bidding Document) forms** — these are provided with each tender and include declarations of interest, past supply chain practices, etc.
- **Technical proposal** addressing the specific tender specifications
- **Pricing schedule** as per the tender format
- **Proof of relevant experience** and reference letters
- **MIOS compliance** — Minimum Interoperability Standards for government IT (SITA publishes these)
- **MISS compliance** — Minimum Information Security Standards
- **Professional indemnity insurance** (often required for IT services contracts)

### B-BBEE Considerations

B-BBEE is not legally mandatory to register on CSD, but it is **practically essential** for competitiveness. Government procurement uses a **Preferential Procurement Framework** where B-BBEE status directly affects scoring:

- **80/20 preference system** (for contracts up to R50 million): 80 points for price, 20 points for B-BBEE
- **90/10 preference system** (for contracts above R50 million): 90 points for price, 10 points for B-BBEE

Without B-BBEE compliance, a company has a very limited chance of competing for government procurement.

## 5. Fees

### CSD Registration: FREE

Registration on the Central Supplier Database is **free of charge**. It is an electronic, self-service process with no registration fees.

### SITA Registration: No Separate Fee

Since SITA relies on CSD for supplier data, there is no additional SITA registration fee.

### Technology Certification Process (TCP): Unclear

The TCP documentation does not publicly specify fees. Contact tas@sita.co.za for current pricing if applicable.

### Third-Party Assistance

Some consulting firms offer CSD registration assistance for fees ranging from R250 to R2,500+, but this is optional — you can do it yourself at no cost.

### Tender Participation: Generally Free

Responding to SITA tenders is typically free, though you may incur costs for:
- Preparing bid documents
- Attending compulsory briefing sessions (travel costs)
- Obtaining required certifications or assessments

## 6. Timeline

### CSD Registration Timeline

| Phase | Duration |
|---|---|
| Online form completion | 1-2 hours |
| Automated verification (SARS, CIPC, Home Affairs, bank) | 5-10 business days |
| Bank verification (manual component) | Can take up to 10 business days |
| **Total typical timeline** | **5-10 business days** |

Some third-party services claim 24-48 hour turnaround, but the bank verification step often takes the longest.

**Important:** CSD registration does not expire. However, you must keep your information current and updated.

### SITA Tender Response Timeline

- SITA publishes tenders with a minimum **21 days** for response (typically 30-45 days)
- Bid evaluation can take **30-90 days** depending on complexity
- Contract award and negotiation: additional **30-60 days**
- For transversal contracts (like RFB 1183), the refresh/re-opening cycle happens periodically — the most recent RFB 1183 refresh opened on **4 February 2025** and closed on **28 February 2025**

### Getting on a Transversal Contract

Transversal contracts are awarded through competitive bidding and typically have multi-year terms (3-5 years). You can only join when SITA issues a new RFB or refreshes an existing contract. The supplier list is refreshed annually by SITA.

## 7. SITA Transversal Contracts

### What Are Transversal Contracts?

Transversal contracts are **centralised, multi-department agreements** that National Treasury and SITA facilitate for goods or services that multiple government institutions require. They are:

- Negotiated centrally to leverage government's collective purchasing power
- Mandatory for government departments to use (where applicable)
- Available to all national departments, provincial departments, parastatals, and organs of state
- Designed to standardise pricing, terms, and quality across government

### Legal Basis

- **Public Finance Management Act (PFMA)**
- **SITA Act (No. 88 of 1998)**
- **Section 217 of the Constitution** — requiring fair, equitable, transparent, competitive, and cost-effective procurement

### Currently Active Transversal Contracts

#### Hardware Contracts
- **RFB 740** — Hardware procurement (PCs, servers, etc.)
- **RFB 2004** — OEM provision of Personal Computers, mobile devices, and services
- **RFB 2005** — Reseller provision of Personal Computers, mobile devices, and services
- **RFB 2007** — Reseller provision of Peripherals, Consumables, and Services
- **RFB 2003** — Servers and Storage equipment, Solutions, and Services

#### Software Contracts
- **Microsoft Enterprise Agreement** — Runs July 2022 to June 2027 (for Microsoft licensing across government)
- Other software contracts are available but less publicly documented

#### Services Contracts
- **RFB 1183** — Transversal contract for IT services and skills (the primary vehicle for IT consulting, development, security, and identity services)

### Can VeriGate Get on a Transversal Contract?

**Potentially, yes — through RFB 1183.** However:

- You can only apply when SITA opens a **refresher or new bid** for the contract
- The most recent RFB 1183-2024 refresh opened in February 2025 — the next refresh date is unknown
- Suppliers are accredited per **province** and per **service category**
- As a Cape Town company, Arthmatic DevWorks would need to target the **Western Cape** accreditation at minimum
- VeriGate would fit under the **Information Security Services** portfolio (Specialised Identity Management / Access Control)

### Threshold Rules

- For procurements under R500,000 from a transversal contract, departments can engage a single supplier from the panel
- For procurements above R500,000, departments must approach **all suppliers** on the panel for that service category to maintain fairness

### Panel Contracts (Separate from Transversal Contracts)

SITA also maintains **panel contracts** through RFA (Request for Accreditation) processes:

| Panel | Reference | Expiry |
|---|---|---|
| LAN Cabling Services | RFA 2168/2019 | April 2026 |
| Unified Communications & Video Conferencing | RFA 2501-2021 | April 2026 |
| **Information Security Products & Services** | **RFA 2494-2021** | **August 2027** |
| End User Computing Infrastructure Support | RFA 952 | April 2026 |
| Training Provider Database | RFB 1626-2017 | Month-to-month |

The **Information Security Products & Services panel (RFA 2494-2021)** is potentially relevant for VeriGate. It expires in August 2027, and it is unclear whether new suppliers can join mid-term.

### OEM/OSM Framework Agreements

SITA maintains framework agreements with major OEMs (Original Equipment Manufacturers) and OSMs (Original Software Manufacturers). These are typically reserved for large multinational technology companies (Microsoft, Oracle, SAP, etc.) and their authorised resellers.

## 8. SITA's Relationship with the CSD

### The CSD Is the Foundation

The **Central Supplier Database (CSD)** is a National Treasury initiative that serves as the **single source of truth** for all supplier information across all spheres of government. The relationship works as follows:

```
National Treasury
    |
    v
Central Supplier Database (CSD) -- www.csd.gov.za
    |
    +--> Used by SITA for IT procurement
    +--> Used by all national departments
    +--> Used by all provincial departments
    +--> Used by municipalities
    +--> Used by SOEs and public entities
```

### Do You Need CSD First?

**Yes, absolutely.** CSD registration is a **prerequisite** for any engagement with SITA:

- You cannot respond to any SITA RFQ or RFB without a CSD number
- Your CSD number and unique reference code must appear on every bid submission
- SITA's procurement officials verify your CSD status, tax compliance, and B-BBEE level directly through the CSD system during bid evaluation

### How They Interact

1. **You register on CSD** (self-service, once)
2. **SITA publishes a tender** on its website and the eTender portal
3. **You respond to the tender**, including your CSD number
4. **SITA evaluates your bid** and checks your CSD information (tax compliance, B-BBEE status, company validity) in real-time during evaluation
5. **CSD verification happens automatically** — procurement officers can verify your status without you needing to resubmit tax clearance certificates or B-BBEE documents each time

### Key Benefit

Once registered on CSD, you are visible to **all** government entities, not just SITA. This means your CSD registration opens doors to:

- Direct departmental procurement (where departments are permitted to procure independently)
- Municipal procurement
- SOE procurement (Eskom, Transnet, etc.)
- Provincial government procurement

## 9. Recent Changes (2024-2026)

### Major Reform: Departments Can Now Procure IT Independently

The most significant change affecting IT suppliers in 2025-2026 is the **amendment to SITA regulations** by Minister Solly Malatsi (DCDT):

- **Previously:** Government departments were strictly required to procure all IT through SITA. Procurement outside SITA was flagged as irregular expenditure.
- **Now (from mid-2025):** Departments have been empowered to **procure digital services independently** where there is a compelling business case for better value and faster turnaround.

#### How the New Rules Work

1. A department identifies an IT need
2. The department **must still approach SITA first**
3. The department provides SITA with a business case, user requirement specifications, and a required delivery timeline
4. SITA has **10 working days** to respond, indicating whether it can fulfill the requirement within the specified timeframe and budget
5. If SITA **cannot meet the requirement**, the department may procure independently through standard PFMA procurement processes
6. The department must demonstrate: faster turnaround time, lower cost, and compliance with procurement prescripts

#### Why This Happened

This reform was driven by:

- Mounting governance concerns at SITA
- Irregular procurement practices within SITA itself
- Operational inefficiencies and alarming deterioration in service delivery
- SITA being a bottleneck that delayed critical government IT projects

#### What This Means for Arthmatic DevWorks

This is **very good news**. It means:

- Departments can now approach VeriGate **directly** (after giving SITA first right of refusal)
- The previous SITA monopoly bottleneck is loosening
- Departments frustrated with SITA's slow turnaround times now have a legal pathway to work with smaller, agile IT companies
- Building relationships directly with department CIOs and IT managers becomes a viable strategy
- VeriGate's **conflict of interest detection** is particularly compelling for departments under Auditor-General scrutiny for procurement irregularities and undisclosed relationships -- it directly addresses a governance gap that no existing SITA-contracted solution fills

### Automated Procurement Platform

SITA has soft-launched an **automated supply chain management platform** to:

- Streamline procurement workflows
- Incorporate predictive analytics
- Reduce administrative overhead
- Provide real-time reporting and audit trails
- Improve supplier engagement with streamlined onboarding

### g-Commerce Platform

SITA and National Treasury developed the **g-Commerce platform** — a fully integrated source-to-pay solution for government procurement. It provides:

- An e-catalogue portal for transversal contracts
- Improved tracking of transactions and buying patterns
- Better price visibility and contract management

### Digitech SMME Platform

The DCDT and SITA established the **Digitech SMME platform**, which:

- Provides an inclusive marketplace for SMMEs to showcase products and services
- Connects SMMEs with government and private sector customers
- Aims to expand reach locally and globally

### SITA Innovation Centre

Located at SITA's Centurion offices, the Innovation Centre:

- Provides facilities for South African innovators and SMMEs in the ICT industry
- Creates avenues for collaboration and development
- Supports the ICT SMME Development Strategy

### GovTech 2025 Conference

At GovTech 2025, SITA unveiled new digital projects including:

- Plans for a **Citizen Super App** for government services
- Strategic partnerships with **Microsoft** and DCDT for paperless government
- Continued digital transformation of public services

## 10. Alternative and Complementary Routes

### Route 1: Direct Departmental Engagement (NEW — Post-2025 Reform)

With the amended SITA regulations, departments can now procure IT independently after giving SITA first right of refusal. This creates a direct-to-department sales channel:

- **Identify target departments** that have verification needs (Home Affairs, SAPS, Justice, Social Development, etc.)
- **Build relationships** with CIOs, IT directors, and procurement heads
- **Position VeriGate** as a solution to a specific departmental problem
- The department can then follow the SITA "opt-out" process if SITA cannot deliver

### Route 2: eTender Portal Monitoring

Register and actively monitor the **eTender Publication Portal** ([www.etenders.gov.za](https://www.etenders.gov.za/)):

- All public sector tenders across national, provincial, and municipal levels are published here
- Set up alerts for keywords: "verification," "identity," "gateway," "API," "software," "SaaS," "security"
- Also monitor the transparency portal at data.etenders.gov.za

### Route 3: Provincial Government Procurement

Provincial departments (especially Western Cape, where Arthmatic is based) often have their own IT procurement processes:

- Western Cape Government has its own e-procurement system
- Provincial departments can procure within their delegated authority thresholds
- Building local government relationships in the Western Cape is strategically sound

### Route 4: Municipal Procurement

Municipalities are **not bound by the SITA Act** (SITA's mandate covers national and provincial government). Municipalities can:

- Procure IT directly from their own supplier databases
- Use their own tender processes
- Metro municipalities (Cape Town, Johannesburg, etc.) have substantial IT budgets
- Register on municipal supplier databases directly

### Route 5: SOE and Public Entity Procurement

State-owned enterprises and public entities (SARS, CIPC, Companies Tribunal, NPA, etc.) may have their own procurement processes:

- Many SOEs maintain separate supplier registration portals
- SOEs can procure independently or through SITA
- Entities like SARS (who already deal with verification extensively) could be strong targets for VeriGate

### Route 6: Subcontracting to SITA Transversal Contract Holders

If Arthmatic cannot immediately get onto a transversal contract:

- Partner with or subcontract through a company already on the RFB 1183 panel
- Many larger IT companies on SITA panels need specialised technology partners
- This gives VeriGate exposure to government projects without direct SITA accreditation

### Route 7: Public-Private Partnerships (PPPs)

For larger, longer-term engagements:

- National Treasury's PPP Unit facilitates partnerships where private entities deliver services
- Suitable for ongoing verification services delivered as a managed service
- Contact: National Treasury PPP Unit, Tel: +27 12 315 5741

### Route 8: SITA Innovation Centre / Digitech SMME

Engage with SITA's SMME development initiatives:

- Apply to showcase VeriGate at the SITA Innovation Centre (Centurion)
- Register on the Digitech SMME platform
- Participate in GovTech events and SITA-hosted innovation showcases

## 12. Tips and Common Pitfalls

### Tips for Arthmatic DevWorks

1. **Register on CSD immediately** — This is free, takes 5-10 business days, and is the non-negotiable first step. Do not wait until you see a relevant tender.

2. **Keep CSD information current** — Procurement officials verify your information in real-time during bid evaluation. Outdated tax compliance or incorrect banking details will disqualify you automatically.

3. **Get your B-BBEE right** — As an SMME, you can use a sworn affidavit (free) if your turnover is below R10 million (EME) or a verification certificate if between R10-50 million (QSE). A strong B-BBEE score (Level 1-4) is effectively a prerequisite for government work.

4. **Select UNSPSC codes carefully on CSD** — Choose codes that match VeriGate's capabilities: identity verification, access control, software services, security consulting. These codes determine which tender notifications you receive.

5. **Monitor tenders actively** — Set up daily monitoring of sita.co.za/content/invitations and etenders.gov.za. Relevant tenders can have 21-45 day response windows — you need to spot them early.

6. **Attend compulsory briefing sessions** — Many SITA tenders have compulsory briefing sessions. Missing one disqualifies your bid.

7. **Build department relationships before the tender** — Government procurement is relationship-driven. Identify departments that need verification services (Home Affairs, SAPS, DHA, Social Development, SARS) and engage their IT leadership. When a tender is published, you want to already be a known entity.

8. **Position VeriGate within SITA's language** — Frame your product using SITA's terminology: "Specialised Identity Management Services," "Specialised Access Control Services," "Business Solution Development." Match the RFB 1183 service definitions.

9. **Consider subcontracting initially** — Partner with a company already on the RFB 1183 transversal contract. This gets VeriGate into government projects faster while you build your own track record.

10. **Leverage the 2025 SITA reform** — The new regulations allowing departments to procure independently are a significant opportunity for agile SMMEs. Target departments frustrated with SITA's turnaround times.

11. **Comply with MIOS and MISS** — Ensure VeriGate meets the Minimum Interoperability Standards and Minimum Information Security Standards. Non-compliance is a disqualifier.

12. **Prepare a standard government response template** — Government bids require extensive documentation (SBD forms, declarations, technical proposals). Having templates ready saves time when a relevant tender drops.

### Common Pitfalls

1. **Assuming SITA has a separate supplier register** — They do not. CSD is the register. Do not waste time looking for a SITA-specific registration form.

2. **Expired tax compliance** — SARS TCS status is verified in real-time. If your tax affairs are not in order, your bid is automatically disqualified regardless of technical merit.

3. **Incorrect or outdated CSD information** — Non-existing or outdated supplier records are a known problem on CSD. Keep your profile active and accurate.

4. **Missing the transversal contract window** — RFB 1183 refreshes happen periodically (not on a fixed schedule). If you miss the application window, you may wait 1-2 years for the next one. Monitor SITA's procurement page constantly.

5. **Underestimating B-BBEE impact** — The 20-point (or 10-point) B-BBEE scoring component can make or break a bid. A Level 1 B-BBEE contributor gets maximum preference points.

6. **Not attending compulsory briefing sessions** — This is an automatic disqualification. There is no exception.

7. **Submitting late** — Government procurement has strict deadlines. Late submissions are not accepted under any circumstances.

8. **Not understanding the threshold rules** — Different procurement rules apply based on value: RFQs for lower values, full tender processes for higher values. The R500,000 threshold on transversal contracts triggers different competitive requirements.

9. **Ignoring provincial presence requirements** — SITA transversal contracts accredit suppliers per province. If you are registered only in Western Cape, you may not be eligible for Gauteng-based opportunities. Consider whether to seek multi-province accreditation.

10. **Treating government procurement like private sector sales** — Government procurement is process-driven, compliance-heavy, and slow. Patience, documentation rigour, and procedural adherence are essential.
